Picture Name Description
Crisóstomo Ibarra Ibarra, a wealthy young mestizo who has recently returned to the Philippines after studying in Europe for seven years, is sophisticated, highly esteemed, and idealistic.Because of his liberal education and connections, the priests of San Diego are all wary of him.
Don Rafael Ibarra In the novel, Crisóstomo Ibarra's father is mentioned posthumously. He enrages Father Dámaso, who accuses him of sedition and heresy for criticizing the corrupt practices of the Spanish friars.
Father Dámaso Father Dámaso is an elderly, ruthlessly corrupt, and Spanish priest who has spent almost two decades residing among the native Filipinos. Despite spending so much time with them, the years have done nothing to make him like them or give him any sympathy for his "flock."
Captain Tiago Because he is a prosperous native-born Filipino, Capitan Tiago is a rarity. Despite the fact that he has no respect for religion, he maintains close relationships with prominent Catholics and shamelessly participates in racial slurs directed at his own people. His main priority is finding a wealthy, powerful man to marry off his daughter, Mara Clara.
Maria Clara She is a woman of high social standing and is believed to be Father Dámaso's goddaughter as well as the daughter of Capitan Tiago. She is actually Father Dámaso's biological child, the result of their scandalous relationship with Capitan Tiago's wife.
